The scene rises around you, displacing the Styx. You stand in a dimly lit kitchen. A younger version of yourself sits at a table, poring over a stack of bills. Across the room, a woman stands with arms crossed, frustration etched into her face.

"You're always here, but you're never really here,” she says. “Do you even care anymore?”

Your younger self doesn't look up. His voice is low, tired. "You know I'm working all the time, I can't be the caregiver. Someone has to keep the lights on." The words hang in the air like smoke, choking and bitter.

"She knew you were lying every time. You always took on those hours to get on good terms with that lovely supervisor of yours."
